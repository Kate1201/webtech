My native city is Donetsk.
Here are some interesting facts about it:

  • The first work on the construction of the coast of Kalmius metallurgical plant and the village under the leadership of the British John James Hughes (Hughes) was in the autumn of 1870. At the same time, the million-plus city Yuzovka village grew around the factory.
  • Yuzovka acquired the status of the city only in 1917, due to which Donetsk is one of the youngest cities in Ukraine.
  • Donetsk metro began to be built in the distant 1993. However, the construction did not go according to plan. More precisely, it did not go at all. The problem turned out to be mine workings and groundwater. On the eve of the European Football Championship, it became clear that the subway would not be in time. Then they decided to build either a mini-subway or a monorail. Although the locals are sure: the subway is not needed.
  • Donetsk is also sometimes called "the city of a million roses" . Planting roses in Donetsk was planned even before World War II, and since the 50s, when the city’s landscaping program was adopted, this process has gained particular momentum.
  • Also there is a park of forged figures in my native town. The exhibits of blacksmithing standing in the open air are not unique and may be found in many countries. But only in Donetsk they are grouped on the territory of a dedicated park, constantly replenished (even now) and form a kind of thematic unity - more precisely, even several thematic avenues.
